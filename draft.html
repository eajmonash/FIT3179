Introduction

Explore the geography of school shootings across the United States and gain insights into annual trends with our visualizations. The map displays locations of school shootings, while a bar chart shows the annual counts of incidents, including the number of casualties.Dive into the data to better understand the impact of these tragic events over time.

Map of the United States with shooting locations

Number of shooting in per state

This map depicts school shootings that happened in the United States between 1998 and 2023.
Each marker represents a different shooting. Exploring this map allows you to discover
the geographic patterns of these incidents and where they are most significant.
This visualization serves as an important reminder of the issue's worldwide scale as well
as the importance of ongoing awareness and action.

Annual numbers of shootings

This bar chart visualization presents an overview of school shootings in the United States over the years.Each bar represents a different year and displays the overall number of casualties, including both fatalities and injuries. This graphic makes it simple to identify trends and patterns in the frequency and severity of school shootings. The donut chart that accompanies with the bar chart looks at the gender distribution of the shooters.It divides shooters into three categories: male, female, and unknown, giving the percentage of each gender engaged in school shootings. This representation sheds light on the gender patterns of school shooters. These visualizations, when put together, provide a full understanding of the history of school shootings, from yearly patterns in victims to the gender distribution of perpetrators. Understanding these characteristics is critical for dealing with and preventing such unfortunate events.

Relation between the age of the shooter and the shooting type

In this boxplot visualization, the relationship between shooter age and shooting types is examined.
Each boxplot represents a different type of shooting, showing the distribution of shooter ages within each category.
The vertical axis displays shooter age, while the horizontal axis represents shooting types.
By comparing these boxplots, it is possible to identify age patterns among different shooting types.
Look for variations in age range and possible outliers, which may reveal unique trends or anomalies within the data.
This visualization offers valuable insights into shooter age dynamics across various shooting incidents, aiding in the development of prevention and policy strategies.
